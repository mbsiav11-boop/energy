Understanding Energy in Everyday Life

Energy powers everything around us — from devices in our homes to entire cities. Understanding scale begins with understanding use.

How Everyday Activities Compare

The average U.S. home consumes approximately 29 kilowatt-hours (kWh) per day.

Understanding the Units:
• 1,000 Watts (W) = 1 kilowatt (kW)
• kWh measures energy used over time.
• Formula: Energy (kWh) = (Watts × Hours) ÷ 1000
Energy Used: 2 kWh

Fun Facts:
  • 🔵 Heating & Cooling = ~40% of home energy.
  • 🟡 Water heaters operate daily.
  • 🟢 LEDs use up to 75% less electricity.
  • ⚡ Solar panels can offset part of 29 kWh/day.
