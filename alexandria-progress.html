How Alexandria Tracks Energy Use and Carbon Emissions

Cities use large amounts of energy to power homes, transportation, and infrastructure. When fossil fuels are burned to generate electricity, carbon dioxide (CO₂) is released. Cities track this pollution in metric tons of CO₂ to measure climate progress.

Understanding Metric Tons

1 metric ton = 1,000 kilograms of CO₂.

  • 1 kg CO₂ ≈ driving a gasoline car about 2–3 miles
  • 100 kg CO₂ ≈ driving about 250 miles
  • 1 metric ton CO₂ ≈ driving about 2,500 miles
  • ~4 metric tons ≈ average yearly home electricity emissions

How Alexandria's Energy Use Has Changed

  • Alexandria measures emissions in million metric tons of CO₂.
  • The city uses 2005 as its baseline year for tracking progress.
  • The goal is to reduce emissions 50% below 2005 levels by 2030.
  • Long-term plans aim for net-zero emissions by 2050.
  • Progress has already been made through efficiency upgrades and cleaner electricity.

Getting Involved in Alexandria

Students and teens can play an important role in helping Alexandria reduce energy use and carbon emissions. Small actions in schools, neighborhoods, and city programs can make a real difference when many people participate.

Join School Environmental Clubs

Many Alexandria schools have sustainability or environmental clubs. Students work on projects like reducing energy waste, organizing recycling programs, and promoting climate awareness.

Participate in City Clean-Up Events

The City of Alexandria regularly hosts community clean-up days where volunteers help remove trash from parks and waterways. Keeping public spaces clean helps protect local ecosystems.

Use Sustainable Transportation

Walking, biking, or using public transportation instead of driving reduces emissions from transportation, one of the largest sources of city carbon pollution.

Volunteer with Local Environmental Groups

Organizations in Alexandria offer volunteer opportunities focused on tree planting, energy awareness campaigns, and climate education.

Support Energy Conservation at Home

Teens can help families reduce electricity use by turning off lights, using energy-efficient appliances, and being aware of daily energy use.

Attend City Sustainability Events

Alexandria hosts sustainability workshops and climate planning meetings where residents can learn about city goals and contribute ideas for reducing emissions.
